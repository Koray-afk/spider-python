What is Breeze, HubSpot's AI?

Breeze is HubSpot's AI, designed to help you generate high-quality content, automate tedious tasks, and boost productivity by leveraging your CRM data. It’s built into HubSpot’s platform to provide contextualized AI experiences across marketing, sales, and customer service.

What can Breeze Agents do for my business?

Breeze agents can help your business in several ways, including automating landing page creation, personalizing outreach to leads, resolving customer inquiries instantly, enriching data, and generating various types of content based on your CRM data and brand guidelines.

How is Breeze different from standalone AI tools like ChatGPT or Copilot?

Unlike standalone AI tools, Breeze is natively integrated into your HubSpot CRM, meaning it has direct access to your customer data. This allows it to generate more relevant, personalized, and accurate outputs that are deeply contextualized to your business, brand, and customer interactions.

Is Breeze secure and compliant with enterprise data standards?

Yes, Breeze is built with enterprise-grade security. HubSpot enforces least-privilege access controls, encryption, logging, and monitoring. Third-party AI providers are contractually prohibited from training their models on your data, and you maintain control over what data AI can access. HubSpot also publishes model cards for transparency.

How much does Breeze cost, and is it included in my HubSpot plan?

Some Breeze features are included in various HubSpot plans (Free, Starter, Professional, Enterprise). Certain advanced AI Agents like Customer Agent, Prospecting Agent, and Data Agent are charged on a usage-based model (e.g., per resolution, per lead, per answer) and run on HubSpot Credits, with select plans including a monthly credit allowance. Refer to the pricing section for details.

Which HubSpot plan includes AEO and the AI agents?

HubSpot AEO is included in the Professional and Enterprise plans. AI Agents like Prospecting Agent and Data Agent are available starting with the Starter plan, while Customer Agent is available from the Professional plan. More advanced features and agents are generally found in higher-tier plans (Professional and Enterprise).
